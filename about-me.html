My work method

In general, I like to be involved from the start of a project. Taking part of the early stages of a project allows me to be of value immediately to catch issues and chose the right development technique.

When working in teams I find it important to work closely with designers in an iterative manner. This allows us the flexibility to explore ideas more easily and find our way to solutions that work using each others capabilities.

Library

A collection of links to things I like to read and listen to.

Books

Design is a job

Mike Monterio goes deep into the web designer work experience. Writing how both fun and terrifying it can be when working with customers while working at an agency or as a freelancer. He shares important tips like how not be an asshole and how you can make sure you value what you are doing.

www.abookapart.com

CSS Secrets

CSS expert Lea Verou provides loads of tips and tricks to get through everyday design tasks, that should be simple but are not with CSS. The book is filled with well-written DRY, maintainable and flexible code examples.

www.oreilly.com

The Manual

The Manual is a thrice-yearly publication with stories from six people working somehow within the web design community. The book does not contain any code, screenshots or tutorials. It’s stories from real life that connects to the work design business in the most fascinating ways.

www.themanual.org

Podcasts

The Web Ahead

Jen Simmons interviews guests about the future of the web. The topics are always very different and it feels like she always gets the most interesting people that are really passionate about a specific topic or technique.

www.5by5.tv/webahead

ShopTalk

Chris Coyier and Dave Rupert has a fun and educating podcast with lots of jokes, sound effects and great guests talking about everything front-end development related.

www.shoptalkshow.com

The Big Web Show

The king of internet Jeffrey Zeldman interviews people about the web industry about web publication, content strategy, typography, web techniques and much more.

muleradio.net/thebigwebshow
