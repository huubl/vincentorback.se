About me

Hi there!

I’m Vincent Orback and I work as a designer and developer in Stockholm, Sweden. I’m currently working at the digital agency Daytona.

Design and technology has always been two of my main interests and with both these passions, working as a web designer makes for a great combination. After studying system development and computer science I’ve been freelancing and worked at a couple of different agencies. Right now I’m working at one of Sweden’s best digital agencies where I work with clients such as Coop and Systembolaget.

When I’m not working with clients I like to do side projects to keep my creativity and code-brain going.

You can read more about what I do and have done in my resume.

Books

Design is a job

Mike Monterio goes deep into the web designer work experience, writing how fun or terrifying it can be when working with customers, both working at an agency or as a freelancer. He shares tips like how important it is to not be an asshole and how you can make sure you get paid for what your doing.

www.abookapart.com

The Manual

The Manual is a thrice-yearly publication with stories from six people working as designers, developers, etc. Stories that explore what we do and who we are as designers. The book does not contain any code, screenshots or tutorials. It’s just stories from real life that connects to the work design business in the most fascinating ways.

www.themanual.org

Conversations with Design Entrepreneurs

This book has it all. Great interviews with some of the smartest, most interesting and driven people whom all got their own start-up. The book is filled with beautiful illustrations and it’s just so good looking. Buy it just to touch it, it’s worth it!

www.kernandburnbook.com

Podcasts

The Web Ahead

Jen Simmons interviews guests every week about the future of the web. The subjects are very different each week and it feels like she always gets the most interesting people that are really passionate about a specific topic or technique.

www.5by5.tv/webahead

ShopTalk

Chris Coyier and Dave Rupert has a fun and educating podcast with loads of jokes, sound effects and great guests talking about everything front-end development related.

www.shoptalkshow.com

The Big Web Show

The king of internet Jeffrey Zeldman interview different people about the web industry about web publication, content strategy, typography, web techniques and much more.

muleradio.net/thebigwebshow

My work method

In general, I’d like to be involved from the start of a project. Taking part of the early stages of a project allows me to be of value immediately to catch issues and chose the right development technique.

I find it important to work closely with designers in an iterative manner. This allows us the flexibility to explore ideas more easily and find our way to solutions that work using each others capabilities.
