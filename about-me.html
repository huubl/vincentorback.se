About me

Hi there!

My name is Vincent and I work as a designer and developer. Right now I live in Stockholm, Sweden and work at the digital agency Daytona.

Design and technology has always been my main interests and with both these passions web designer is the best job for me! After studying system development and computer science I’ve been freelancing and worked at different web agencies. Right now I’m working at one of Sweden’s best digital agencies where I work with clients such as Coop and Systembolaget.

When I’m not working with clients I like to do some side projects to keep my creativity and code-brain going.

You can read more about what I do and have done in my resume.

Books

Design is a job

Mike Monterio goes deep into the web designer work experience, writing how fun or terrifying it can be when working with customers, both working at an agency or freelancer. He share tips like how important it is to not be an asshole and how you can make sure you get paid for what your doing.

www.abookapart.com

The Manual

The Manual is a thrice-yearly publication with stories from six people working as designers, developers, etc. Stories that explore what we do and who we are as designers. The book does not contain any code, screenshots or tutorials. It’s just stories from real life that connects to the work design business in the most fascinating ways.

www.alwaysreadthemanual.com

Conversations with Design Entrepreneurs

This book has it all. Amazing interviews with some of the smartest, most interesting and driven people whom all got their own start-up. The book is filled with beautiful illustrations and it’s just so good looking. Buy it just to touch it, it’s worth it!

www.kernandburnbook.com

My work method

In general, I’d like to be involved from the start of a project. Taking part of the early stages of a project allows me to be of value immediately to catch issues and chose the right development technique. I find it important to work closely with designers in an iterative manner. This allows us the flexibility to explore ideas more easily and find our way to solutions that work using each others capabilities.
